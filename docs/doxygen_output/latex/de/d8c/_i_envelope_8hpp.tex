\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
Thermal envelope (blanket) interface\+: maps base temperature Tb to surface temperature Ts. 

This header defines the abstract interface for neutron-\/star envelope/blanket models. Envelope models provide the thermal boundary condition connecting the nearly-\/isothermal interior to the radiating surface\+:


\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item The interior is typically evolved in terms of a single temperature variable (e.\+g., redshifted temperature T∞ or a local core temperature).
\item The outer envelope (ρ ≲ 10$^\wedge$10 g cm$^\wedge$-\/3, order-\/of-\/magnitude) is not evolved explicitly in the ODE system; instead its effect is encoded in a phenomenological/fit mapping Tb → Ts that depends on surface gravity and composition (light-\/element content).
\end{DoxyItemize}

Typical usage\+:
\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item Photon\+Cooling evaluates Ts(\+Tb, g14, xi) and then computes Lγ,∞.
\item The implementation choice (iron vs accreted vs custom) is injected via options/config.
\end{DoxyItemize}

Conventions\+:
\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item Tb and Ts are {\bfseries{local-\/frame}} temperatures \mbox{[}K\mbox{]} (not redshifted).
\item g14 is surface gravity in units of 10$^\wedge$14 cm s$^\wedge$-\/2.
\item xi is a model-\/dependent “light-\/element” parameter. Its meaning depends on the chosen envelope prescription (e.\+g., column depth, composition proxy, etc.).
\end{DoxyItemize}

This interface is intentionally small and pure; it carries no ownership of star structure, geometry caches, or EOS. Those belong in higher-\/level objects (Star\+Context/\+Geometry\+Cache) and are used by drivers to compute inputs like g14 or to decide what Tb represents.
